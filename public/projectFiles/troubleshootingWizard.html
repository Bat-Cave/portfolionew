Alder Simple Door/Window Sensor

Checklist

Have you located the sensor?
Have you removed the cover from the larger sensor piece?
Have you put the sensor back on the mount?

Troubleshooting Progress

0%

Troubleshooting

  • There are 2 parts to the door sensor. The larger part contains the circuit board and tamper switch.
  • Remove the sensor from the mount.
    • Look for the three white lines on the top or the bottom of the sensor.
    • There will be a small gap between the sensor and the mount on the side with the lines.
    • Place your finger in the gap and push out to unclip the sensor for the mount.
    • Use a butter knife, flathead screwdriver, or a key if you can't get it with your finger.
  • Clip sensor back onto the mount.
  • All troubleshooting complete. If the sensor is still reading Tamper, replace the sensor.

2GIG Image Sensor

Checklist

Have you located the detector?
Have you removed the detector from the mount?
Have you put the detector back on the mount?
Have you waited 5 minutes for the tamper to clear?

Troubleshooting Progress

0%

Troubleshooting

  • Locate the image sensor. Image sensors are usually mounted 6-7 feet high on a wall.
  • Slide the sensor up towards the ceiling.
  • Slide the sensor back down onto the mount.
  • A built-in accelerometer detects movement or re-positioning of the Image Sensor. Image Sensors will tamper whenever a change in sensor orientation is detected. 5 minutes of no movement will clear the tamper.
  • All troubleshooting complete. If the sensor is still reading Tamper, replace the sensor.

2GIG Smoke Detector

Checklist

Have you located the detector?
Have you removed the detector from the mount?
Have you put the detector back on the mount?

Troubleshooting Progress

0%

Troubleshooting

  • Locate the smoke detector. Common locations include: hallway, living room, bedroom.
  • Turn the detector to the left (counter-clockwise) and it should come off the base.
    • The detector will only twist about a ½ an inch.
    • If the detector is too tight to twist by hand, put a butter knife or pen through the slots on the bottom of the smoke and use that as leverage to twist it.
  • Place the detector back on the mount and turn clockwise to secure it again. Once fully mounted, the triangle on the base should align with the triangle on the detector.
  • All troubleshooting complete. If the detector is still reading Tamper, replace the detector.
